Maple Miso Salmon

I love making salmon because it is very difficult to ruin and is delicious. Miso is something I'd never heard of but packs so much power and is an amazing addition to salmon. Serves great with roasted sesame broccoli.

  • 4 (6oz) skiin-on salmon fillets
  • salt+pepper
  • 4 tsp maple syrup
  • 1 Tbsp miso
  • 1 Tbsp rice wine vinegar
  • 2 tsp soy sauce
  • 1 garlic clove, grated
  • 1/4 cup roughly chopped cilantro
  • 4 lime wedges, for serving
  1. Heat the oven to 400 degrees and line a sheet pan with parchment paper or foil. Seaon the salmon fillets well with salt and pepper and place them on a plate or in a large shallow bowl.
  2. In a small bowl wisk together the maple syrup, miso, rice wine, vinegar, soy sauce, and garlic. Pour the mixture on top of the salmon and gently massage the marinade all over the fish. Let marinate while the oven comes to temperature.
  3. Arrange the salmon skin-side down on the sheet pan and bake until the salmon is opaque - roughly 12 minutes. Right before serving scatter with cilantro and a good squeeze of lime. Season with flaky salt and serve with rice (optional).
  4. Divide the pad Thai between two plates, then wipe the pan clean and repeat with the remaining ingredients. Scatter with peanutsand coriander and serve with lime wedges.
