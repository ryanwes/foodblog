GunPowder Chicken

This Chinese stir fry will have you grabbing the milk if you can't handle the heat. This dish has a punch of flavor with an infusion of flavor from the red chilies and pefect crunch from the whole roasted peanuts. Enjoy!

  • 8 boneless, skinless ckicken thighs, quartered
  • 1 Tbs soy sauce
  • 1 Tbs shaoxing rice wine
  • 2 Tbs vegetable oil
  • 10 dried long red chillies, stems and seeds removed
  • 1 bunch spring onions (scallions), cut into 2.5cm 1 inch pieces
  • 40g whole roasted peanut
  • 1 heaped tps sichuan peppercorns, ground
Sauce
  • 60ml (1/4 cup) soy sauce
  • 60ml (1/4 cup) Chinese black vinegar or red rice vinegar
  • 60ml (1/4 cup) chicken stock
  • 1 Tbs shaoxing rice wine or dry sherry
  • 2 tsp caster (superfine) sugar
  • 2 tsp cornflour (cornstarch), mixed with 1 Tbs water
  1. Combine the chicken, soy sauce and shaoxing rice wine in a bowl, cover and refrigerate for 1 hour.
  2. Meanwhile to make the sauce, combine all of the ingredients in a small bowl and set aside.
  3. Cook the chicken, in batches, until golden all over, the n set aside. Add the remaining oil and cook the chillies for 1 minute, shaking the wok constantly. Return the chicken to the wok, add the spring onion peanuts and ground sichuan pepper and stir-fry for 1 minute. Add the sauce and cook for another 1-2 minutes or until reduced and thickened. Serve immediately with steamed rice.
