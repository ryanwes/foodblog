Cacio e Pepe

This pasta dish is a wonderful mix of citrus and heat. The flavor explodes through while enjoying this pasta and you will not regret using a heavy hand with the parmesan. Pairs great with a salad (optional).

  • Spaghetti 12oz
  • 1/2lb pancetta or prosciutto, flinely diced
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp red pepper flakes, plus more to taste
  • 2 tsp freshly ground pepper, plus more to taste
  • 1/4 cup fresh lemon juice
  • 1 1/2 cups freshly grated parmesan cheese, plus more for serving
  • 3 cups baby arugula
  1. In a large pot of heavily salted boiling water, cook the spaghetti to al dente according to the package directions. Reserve 1 cup of the pasta water, then drain the pasta.
  2. Meanwhile in a large skillet, cook the prosciutto. Over medium-high heat until crisp. Add the olive oil, then add the garlic, red pepper flakes, black pepper and cook until fragrant.
  3. Add the lemon juice to the skillet, then toss in the drained pasta and toss to coat. Add the parmesan and toss, adding the pasta water a couple of Tbsp at a time, just to help the cheese coat the pasta. Add the arugula and toss until it wilts, about one minute. season to taste with additional salt lots of black pepper, and red pepper flakes. Serve with more parmesan
