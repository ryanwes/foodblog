Skillet Charred Fish Tacos

These tacos are an awesome Summer dinner! Be careful with the green pepper sauce as it really packs a punch. Pairs great with grilled corn.

Spicy Mayo
  • 1/2 cup mayonnaise
  • 1/4 cup sriracha
Fish
  • 3 Tbsp chili powder
  • 2 garlic cloves minced
  • 1 1/2 tsp kosher salt
  • 1/2 tsp freshly ground black pepper
  • 3 Tbsp canola oil
  • 1 1/2 pounds tilapia fillets
Hot Green Pepper Sauce
  • 4 serrano peppers
  • 6 garlic cloves
  • 3 Tbsp fish sauce
  • 1/2 Tbsp brown sugar
    • juice and zest of 1/2 lime
For the Tacos
  • 12 corn tortillas
  • 1 cup finely shredded red cabbage
  • 2 small tomatoes, diced
  • 6 radishes, thinly sliced or julienned
  • 1/2 cup sliced scallions
  • 1 large avocado, sliced
  • Hot Green Pepper Sauce
  • Cilantro sprigs and lime wedges, for serving
Make the Green Pepper Sauce
  1. In a blender comine the peppers, garlic, and 1/4 cup wateer and plse about 15 times to get mixture combined. Then blend until smooth, then transfer to a bowl and stir in the fish sauce, brown sugar, and lime juice.
Make the Spicy Mayo
  1. In a bowl, stir together the mayo and sriracha until smooth.
Make the Fish
  1. In a bowl, combine the chili powder, garlic, salt, pepper, and 1 1/2 Tbsp of the oil. Rub the spice mixture all over the fish. Heat a cast-iron skillet over medium-high heat. Add the remaining 1 1/2 Tbsp oil and when the oil just starts to smoke, add the fish in one layer and cook until slightly charred. 3 minutes per side. Remove from the heat, cool, and flake the fish.
Assemble the Tacos
  1. Warm the tortillas, two by two, in a dry skillet set over medium heat, flipping after a few seconds. Stack the hot tortillas to keep them warm.
  2. Spread 1 Tbsp of the spicy mayo on each tortilla, then top with some fish. Top each taco with some cabbage, tomato, radish, and scallion. Then Top each taco with avocado. Drizzle with some hot green pepper sauce. Garnish with cilantro and serve with lime wedges.
